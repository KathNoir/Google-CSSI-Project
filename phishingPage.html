Phishing & Keylogging

Phishing and keylogging are two of the most common modern cybersecurity threats today. With phishing, individuals or groups will falsely pose as an existing organization in order to incentivize victims into providing personal information, such as credit card information or passwords, either through volunteering or device malware. The same can occur for keylogging where a victim enters a website or clicks a link that unknowingly downloads a virus or other malware on their device and tracks their keyboard input. Both cybercrimes can target individuals via email, phone, or text, and often appear as spam messages.

IF THE OFFER IS:

1) Too good to be true
2) Portrays an odd sense of urgency
3) Has unidentifiable hyperlinks
4) Excessive attachments
5) Unusual or unknown sender or address
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID PHISHING AND KEYLOGGING:

1) Use spam filters for your email inbox
2) Change your passwords multiple times a year (4-5 times)
3) Adjust your web browsing habits! Do not enter websites or click links that appear suspicious or uncredible
4) Make sure the link has a valid Secure Socket Layer (SSL) certificate beginning with “https”
5) Trust your gut! If something does not look right, do not take the risk
