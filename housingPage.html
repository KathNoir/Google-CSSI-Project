Free Resources or Housing

Scams that claim to provide free shelter or resources for homeless individuals involve lying to or manipulating them into committing other acts as compensation for the housing. These can be executed through text, email, or call. An instance of this occurred in New York where two lawyers were incentivizing homeless individuals to undergo unnecessary surgeries and feign physical accidents in order to increase the value of their lawsuits. Such scammers may also claim that they have housing, food, or money available for the homeless individual when they do not.

IF THE OFFER INCLUDES:

1) Demands to lie
2) Possible bodily harm
3) Unnecessary measures
4) No reasoning or explanations provided
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID FREE RESOURCE AND HOUSING SCAMS:

1) Rely on readily accessible food banks and shelters
2) Do not immediately trust resources sent digitally
3) Use caution when interacting with individuals who claim to provide money in exchange for fraudulent activity
