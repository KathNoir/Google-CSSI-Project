Ransom Scams

Ransom scams usually contact individuals in order to cause them to send money or resources by falsely declaring that they are holding a family member or friend hostage. Additionally, they may pose as a family member asking for extra financial help or acting as though they are in a dangerous situation. These may be executed via email, call, or text.

IF THE OFFER HAS:

1) An unrecognized phone number
2) An unrecognized voice
3) Requests for large sums of money
4) Money requests are only accepted through wire transfer
5) The caller that orders you not to contact the phone of the alleged victim
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID RANSOM SCAMS:

1) Ignore unknown phone numbers or emails
2) Ask to speak directly to or have the alleged victim described to you
3) Attempt to contact the alleged victim from another device
4) Notify your local police department
