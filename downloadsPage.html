Untrustworthy Downloads

Free downloads may appear on dedicated file-sharing websites or fully reputable websites. They may draw in the user by supposedly offering a free version of an application or program that normally must be purchased. Thus, a victim may choose to download it and unknowingly install malicious software onto their device, such as viruses, adware, scareware, or spyware.

IF THE OFFER IS:

1) A free version of something that users normally pay for
2) A pop-up on any website
3) On a questionable website with lots of ads
4) A URL does not have https at the beginning to signify a secure browser
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID UNTRUSTWORTHY DOWNLOADS:

1) Use virus protection software
2) It Is free for no reason
3) Exit untrustworthy websites
4) Trust your gut! If something does not look right, do not take the risk.
