Fraudulent Insurance Calls/Emails

Fraudulent companies may attempt to contact individuals for false benefits or compensation on life, auto, health, or property insurance. This can be conducted through spam phone calls, regular mail, or email, and are especially prevalent in attacking elderly populations in order to gain their personal information and utilize it.

IF THE OFFER HAS:

1) False or exaggerated claims
2) Misclassification of damages from natural disasters
3) Claims filed by individuals who are unaffected by the event
4) Cost inflation
5) Not following up on agreements from upfront payments
6) Charity fraud scams that misappropriate funds
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID INSURANCE FRAUDS:

1) Report the scam to your state-sponsored fraud bureau
2) Speak directly with your insurance company if you believe a fraud is taking place
3) Gain information from the National Insurance Crime Bureau (NICB) website
4) Gain information from the Coalition Against Insurance Fraud (CAIF) website
5) Gain information from National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) website
