Free Food Stamps

Fraudulent websites or links may falsely claim to provide food stamps or payments. They take advantage of those in need by obtaining their personal information through voluntary contribution or unknowingly after clicking the link. This can lead to the sharing of private information and other forms of theft. Scammers can also pose as a food stamps office employee and ask you to confirm your credit card information over text or email.

IF THE OFFER IS:

1) From an unreputable sender
2) Too good to be true
3) Asks for excessive personal information
BEWARE OF MALICIOUS CONTENT

WHAT TO DO TO AVOID FOOD STAMP FRAUDS:

1) Delete the message
2) Contact your local Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) office
3) Do not reply to any suspicious messages
